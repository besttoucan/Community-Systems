# Genesis Community Systems Website Build Brief
**Prepared for:** Ohad Savir / Genesis Community Systems LLC  
**Date:** June 23, 2026  
**Purpose:** Provide a complete website strategy and AI-build specification for the new Genesis Community Systems website.

---

## 1. Executive objective

Genesis Community Systems LLC needs a real, credible, bank-facing website that supports outbound email outreach, reassures community-bank recipients that the company is legitimate, and preserves a clear separation from Genesis Core Systems LLC.

The site should not be a placeholder, under-construction page, or passive redirect to Genesis Core Systems. It should stand on its own as the public website for Genesis Community Systems LLC, while acknowledging the broader Genesis platform relationship in a modest, legally accurate way.

The primary website objective is simple:

> Make a community-bank executive feel that Genesis Community Systems is real, credible, relevant, and safe enough to take a 20-minute introductory call.

The website does not need to close the sale. It needs to support trust, reduce confusion, and make the Design Partner conversation feel legitimate.

---

## 2. Strategic principles

### 2.1 Genesis Community Systems must stand on its own

Genesis Community Systems should be presented as the bank-facing entity responsible for community-bank engagement and the Design Partner program. The site should have its own identity, copy, contact details, policies, and calls to action.

Do not leave the site empty. A recipient who receives an email from `genesis@genesiscommunitysystems.com` may visit the domain to verify the sender. A blank or “under construction” page weakens credibility.

### 2.2 Keep Genesis Community Systems and Genesis Core Systems separate

The site should preserve legal, operational, and reputation separation between the two Genesis entities. The email outreach is being sent from Genesis Community Systems, so the landing experience should remain Genesis Community Systems.

Do not automatically redirect Genesis Community Systems visitors to Genesis Core Systems.

Do not make Genesis Community Systems look like a shell or marketing wrapper for Genesis Core Systems.

Do not use language such as “Genesis Core Systems is assisting with marketing.” That sounds weak and may create confusion.

### 2.3 Use a modest relationship disclosure

A limited, truthful relationship reference is appropriate. It should appear on the About page and/or in the footer, not as the primary hero message.

Suggested language:

> Genesis Community Systems LLC and Genesis Core Systems LLC are separate Genesis entities with complementary responsibilities. Genesis Community Systems focuses on U.S. community-bank engagement, including the Design Partner program. Genesis Core Systems supports the broader Genesis core banking platform strategy, product development, and ecosystem relationships.

Add this note:

> Any bank engagement is subject to definitive written agreements with the applicable Genesis entity.

This language should be reviewed by counsel before publishing.

### 2.4 Do not put Genesis Core Systems links in campaign emails for now

The website may include one modest link to Genesis Core Systems, preferably on the About page or in the footer. However, outbound email campaign links should go to Genesis Community Systems, not Genesis Core Systems. This keeps the sender domain, website domain, and landing experience consistent.

Use a clean professional sans-serif font. Good choices:
- Inter
- Helvetica Neue
- Arial
- Source Sans 3
- Avenir-like system font

Use large, clear headings, generous whitespace, and short paragraphs.

Avoid overly decorative fonts.

### 4.4 Design style

The visual tone should be:
- Bank-grade
- Calm
- Modern
- Trustworthy
- Plainspoken
- Executive-friendly
- Not flashy
- Not overly technical

Use cards, simple diagrams, and short sections. Avoid dense walls of text.

Hero visual:

- Avoid stock photos of handshakes, skyscrapers, coins, or generic fintech dashboards.
- Use a clean diagram: Legacy Core Complexity → Genesis Design Partner Process → Validated Path Forward.

### 9.3 Problem section

Section title:

> The core dilemma facing community banks

Body copy:

> Many community banks are expected to deliver modern digital experiences, faster product cycles, stronger data access, and reliable compliance support while operating on core environments that were not designed for today’s expectations. The result is often high cost, fragmented vendor management, slow change, and limited flexibility.

Three cards:
1. **Cost pressure**  
   Core-related expenses can consume budget that could otherwise support growth, service quality, and innovation.

2. **Operational complexity**  
   Multiple vendors, integrations, manual workarounds, and legacy constraints make change expensive and slow.

3. **Conversion risk**  
   Banks know modernization is necessary, but traditional core conversions can feel too disruptive and too final too early.

### 9.4 Genesis approach section

Section title:

> A more practical way to evaluate modernization

Body copy:

> Genesis Community Systems is designed around a staged Design Partner model. The bank participates in a structured process to evaluate fit, validate functionality, test migration readiness, and make an informed conversion decision only after agreed criteria are met.

Three pillars:
1. **Validate before committing**  
   The bank should not be asked to make a blind leap.

2. **Right-sized for community banks**  
   The operating model should match the scale, staffing, and economics of community banking.

3. **One accountable path**  
   The bank needs a clear operating model, defined responsibilities, and a practical implementation process.

### 9.5 Design Partner section

Section title:

> The Genesis Design Partner Program

Body copy:

> Genesis is inviting a limited number of U.S. community banks to explore participation in the Design Partner program. Selected banks may help shape the reference configuration, operating model, and roadmap for a modern community-bank platform.

Benefits:
- Priority input into the community-bank reference model
- A structured validation path before conversion commitment
- Standard implementation, integration, configuration, and customization within agreed Design Partner scope
- A practical operating model intended to reduce core-related cost and complexity
- Direct engagement with Genesis leadership

Important clarification:

> Participation in the process does not mean the bank is making a blind conversion commitment. The intent is to evaluate, test, validate, and move forward only if the agreed criteria are satisfied.

CTA:

> Discuss Design Partner Fit

### 9.6 How the process works section

Section title:

> How the process works

Use a 6-step horizontal or vertical timeline:

1. **Introductory conversation**  
   Genesis and the bank discuss goals, pain points, constraints, and whether the Design Partner path is relevant.

2. **Fit assessment**  
   Genesis evaluates whether the bank’s size, needs, and timeline align with the reference model.

3. **Scope and acceptance criteria**  
   The parties define the Design Partner scope, success criteria, UAT criteria, migration criteria, and decision gates.

4. **Configuration and validation**  
   Genesis prepares the environment and works with the bank to validate processes, workflows, reporting, and integrations.

5. **UAT and migration readiness**  
   The bank tests the environment and migration approach against agreed criteria.

6. **Conversion decision**  
   The bank moves toward conversion only after agreed acceptance criteria are met, subject to definitive agreements.

Add note:

> Final commercial, legal, regulatory, implementation, and operational terms are subject to definitive written agreements.

## 13. FAQ page

Recommended FAQ items:

### What is Genesis Community Systems?

Genesis Community Systems LLC works with U.S. community banks exploring a practical path to core modernization through the Genesis Design Partner program.

### Is Genesis Community Systems the same as Genesis Core Systems?

No. Genesis Community Systems LLC and Genesis Core Systems LLC are separate Genesis entities with complementary responsibilities. Genesis Community Systems focuses on community-bank engagement and Design Partner program activity. Genesis Core Systems supports broader platform strategy, product development, and ecosystem relationships.

### What is the Design Partner program?

The Design Partner program is a structured process for selected community banks to help shape and validate a community-bank operating model before moving toward conversion.

### Does joining the process mean the bank must convert?

No. The intended principle is “commit to the process, not the conversion.” Conversion should move forward only after agreed UAT and migration criteria are met and the applicable definitive agreements are in place.

### Is this a core built from scratch?

Use this answer unless vendor names are approved:

> No. Genesis is building around a proven Tier-1 core foundation and packaging it into a right-sized operating model for U.S. community banks.

### What type of bank is a good fit?

Community banks that are actively evaluating core modernization, cost reduction, operational efficiency, vendor simplification, improved digital capability, or a more disciplined conversion path.

### What happens after we request a conversation?

Genesis will schedule a short introductory discussion to understand your bank’s goals, current core environment, timeline, and whether a Design Partner discussion is appropriate.

### How does Genesis address vendor-risk diligence?

Genesis expects banks to conduct appropriate diligence. The Design Partner process is intended to support diligence through clear scope, documented responsibilities, testing criteria, data/migration planning, and transparent decision gates.

### Are there fees?

Use careful language:

> Commercial terms depend on the agreed Design Partner scope and definitive agreements. Within the agreed Design Partner scope, Genesis may cover standard implementation, integration, configuration, and customization work.

### How do we opt out of emails?

Recipients can use the unsubscribe link in the email or contact Genesis Community Systems at genesis@genesiscommunitysystems.com.

## 19. Security and form requirements

- Use HTTPS only.
- Protect forms from spam using a low-friction method such as reCAPTCHA, hCaptcha, Cloudflare Turnstile, or server-side rate limiting.
- Do not collect sensitive bank data through the website.
- Do not ask for account numbers, customer data, credentials, or confidential information.
- Store form submissions securely.
- Send form notifications only to approved Genesis email addresses.
- Include a warning near the contact form:
