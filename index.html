The problem

Community banks are stuck between two bad options

Keep paying for an old core that is expensive and slow to change, or risk a full conversion to something unproven. Most banks know they need to modernize. Few want to bet the bank to do it.

It costs too much

Core fees and the vendors stacked around them eat into money that could go toward growth and customers.

It's hard to change

Multiple vendors, brittle integrations, and manual workarounds make even small changes slow and expensive.

Conversion feels risky

A traditional core conversion can feel too disruptive and too final, with the bank committing before it has seen proof.

Our approach

Prove it first. Decide second.

Genesis is building on a proven Tier-1 core foundation and packaging it for community banks. Instead of asking you to convert and hope it works, we run a staged process where you test the system against criteria you help set.

You see how it handles your processes, your reporting, and your data before anything is binding. If it does not meet the bar, you walk away. That is the point.

Why now

Waiting is its own risk

Margins are tight, customer expectations keep rising, and the cost of running an aging core does not go down. Doing nothing feels safe, but it quietly deepens your dependence on systems that get harder to leave every year.

The point of this process is to let you look at a real alternative carefully, without committing to it before you have seen it work.

Built for due diligence

We expect your team to do real vendor, security, legal, and regulatory diligence. The process is designed to make that easier by putting the details on the table.

  • Defined scope and decision gates
  • Documented responsibilities on both sides
  • Written testing and migration criteria
  • Support for your security and operational review
  • Clear data and transition planning

The path

Six steps, with a clear exit at every gate

Nothing here pushes you toward conversion. Each step has to earn the next one.

  1. First conversation

    We talk through your goals, your core pain points, and whether this is even relevant for your bank.

  2. Fit check

    We look at your size, needs, and timeline and decide together whether to keep going.

  3. Scope and criteria

    We agree, in writing, on the scope, the success criteria, the testing criteria, and the decision gates.

  4. Setup and validation

    We configure the environment and work with your team to check workflows, reporting, and integrations.

  5. Testing and migration readiness

    Your team tests the system and the migration approach against the criteria you set.

  6. Your decision

    You move toward conversion only if the criteria are met. Final terms are set by written agreements.
